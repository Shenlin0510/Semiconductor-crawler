10. Legacy Process Nodes Going Strong (Source: Semiconductor Engineering) ﹀
While the focus is often on the latest silicon nodes, mature nodes are still in high demand in the semiconductor industry. Die shrinks beyond 20nm are countered by more expensive processing, with mask sets becoming more expensive and advanced nodes requiring more layers. As a result, most foundries and integrated device manufacturers (IDMs) continue to have robust business on older nodes, as there is no need for certain parts to be made at smaller nodes. Advanced nodes also have fewer customers due to the high cost, with the choice of process node being application-dependent. The cost of design and masks are also significantly lower in mature nodes.
